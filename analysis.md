# Sustainability Impact Analysis

## Key Findings 🔎 🧐

### 1. Scale of Impact
**601,740 devices** were repurposed in 2024 which demonstrates the large scale of Intel's sustainability efforts.

- On average, each device saves about **25.7 kWh of energy per year** and
if that's  multiplied with all the devices repurposed in 2024, it adds
up to millions of kilowatt hours saved. That’s enough electricity to power
**hundreds of homes for an entire year.** So even though the savings per device look small, the overall effect is 
huge.

- The CO2 savings are even more significant since they reduced a major 
amount of carbon emissions. **The program saved around 6,768 tons of 
CO2 in one year.** For comparison, the average car produces around 4−5 tons per year so 
that means **Intel is taking around 1,400−1,500 cars off the road for a 
year.**
---

### 2. Device Type Insights
- Laptops contributed slightly more to the total impact
- This was mainly due to **higher volume**, not signicificantly higher efficiency as there were about
twice as many laptops (357,417) repurposed in comparison to Desktops (182,162).

   - Laptops are more common in modern workplaces like schools, 
  businesses, and organizations who prefer more portable devices. 
  Companies also replace laptops more frequently than desktops. 
  Laptops might also be easier to redistribute for education and 
  nonprofit uses.

This indicates that **usage pattern** influences sustainability impact.

--- 

### 3. Device Age VS Impact
- Newer devices were repurposed more frequently
- However, **older devices provided greater energy savings per device**

  - Older devices save more energy on average because they’re usually 
  less energy efficient than new ones. When an older device is 
  repurposed instead of replaced by a brand new one, it avoids the larger 
  energy use and emissions that comes from manufacturing a brand new 
  one, so even though fewer older devices are repurposed, each one 
  creates a larger impact on the environment.  
  
This shows a tradeoff between:
- **Volume (newer devices)**
- **Efficiency (older devices)**

---

## 4. Regional Differences
- Engery savings were similar accross regions
- However, **CO2 savings were highest in Asia**

  - This is likely because of differences in carbon intensity of electricity in 
  each region. Areas that rely more on fossil fuels like coal produce more 
  CO2 per unit of electricity, so saving electricity in those regions 
  prevents more emissions overall.
  
  - Asia probably shows the highest average CO2 savings per device because its
  electricity is  more reliant on carbon intensive resources compared to Europe where
  CO2 savings are lower because they rely more on cleaner energy sources. 

---

## My interpretation 
Even though newer devices dominatte in number, older devices produce greater environmental benefit per unit.

Also, location matters:
- Saving energy in high carbon regions leads to larger CO2 reductions.

---

## Reccommendations for Intel

Intel should:
- Prioritize repurposing older devices
- Focus efforts in regions with high carbon intensity
